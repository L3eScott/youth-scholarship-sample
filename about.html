How We Decide

Our scholarship committee reviews every application carefully. We consider:

  • Genuine financial need. Scholarships go to students whose families can't easily cover the cost on their own.
  • Demonstrated work ethic. Grades aren't everything, but how a student has shown up — at school, at work, in their family — matters.
  • Clear plan. Not a perfect plan. Just a real one. What are you studying, where, and what's it for.
  • Community connection. Some tie to our community. You don't have to be a member of St. James. You just have to be from here.

We don't require a faith statement. We don't require church membership. We do require honesty.

What Mentorship Looks Like

Every scholarship recipient gets paired with a mentor from our church family. This isn't a formal program. It's a person. Someone who:

  • Checks in once a month, however the student wants to be checked in on.
  • Shows up for graduations, milestones, and the hard weeks.
  • Prays for the student by name.
  • Stays in their corner long past the scholarship period.
